# Higgsfield UGC Prompt Templates

> **Who reads this:** Leonardo, when writing chunk texts for ugc-chunks.json.
>
> These are copy-paste scaffolds for common UGC archetypes used in Indian D2C / app marketing.
> Replace `[...]` placeholders with brief-specific copy. Keep each chunk under 8 seconds (≈19 words at 2.4 wps).

---

## Archetype 1: Confessional / Honest Review

**When to use:** Testimonial-style ads, "I tried this and..." format, trust-building creatives.

**Avatar recommendation:** `casual`, `25-35` age band, gender matching target audience.

**Chunk structure:**

| Chunk | Role | Example text |
|---|---|---|
| 1 (hook) | Relatable problem or surprising claim | "Sach bolun toh, mujhe bilkul umeed nahi thi [product] se." |
| 2 (tension) | The moment of doubt or skepticism | "Meri friend ne recommend kiya tha, lekin main soch rahi thi — yeh bhi wahi log hain." |
| 3 (turn) | The thing that changed the mind | "[Specific result] dekha toh main honestly shocked reh gayi." |
| 4 (social proof) | Numbers or validation | "[X] din mein [Y result] — yeh maine khud experience kiya hai." |
| 5 (CTA) | Low-pressure close | "Agar tum bhi try karna chahte ho, link bio mein hai." |

**Tone markers per chunk:**
- Chunk 1: Hesitant, honest → "Sach bolun toh...", "Main ek dum honest hoon..."
- Chunk 2: Skeptical, slightly defensive → "Lekin main soch rahi thi..."
- Chunk 3: Genuinely surprised → "Ek dum shocked", "Yaar seriously..."
- Chunk 4: Confident, data-led → declarative statements
- Chunk 5: Casual, non-pushy → "Agar chahte ho toh..."

## Archetype 2: Day-in-the-Life

**When to use:** Lifestyle products, productivity apps, habit-forming products, aspirational brands.

**Avatar recommendation:** `aspirational` or `professional`, matches product audience demographic.

**Chunk structure:**

| Chunk | Role | Example text |
|---|---|---|
| 1 (hook) | A specific moment in the day | "Subah uthte hi mera pehla kaam hota hai [app/product] check karna." |
| 2 (context) | Why this fits their routine | "[Product] ne meri [morning/evening/work] routine completely badal di." |
| 3 (specific benefit) | One concrete benefit in the routine | "Pehle [old problem]. Ab [new situation]." |
| 4 (result) | The cumulative outcome | "[Time period] mein [measurable result]." |
| 5 (relatability close) | Everyone-can-do-this finish | "Agar main kar sakta/sakti hoon, toh seriously anyone can." |

**Tone markers:**
- Relaxed, first-person narration throughout
- Specific times of day make it feel real ("7 baje", "lunch ke baad")
- Avoid aspirational language that sounds scripted ("Transform your life")

## Archetype 3: Reaction / Before-After

**When to use:** Results-driven products, transformations, skill-building apps, finance/savings tools.

**Avatar recommendation:** `energetic` or `relatable`, age matches target segment.

**Chunk structure:**

| Chunk | Role | Example text |
|---|---|---|
| 1 (hook — the after) | Start with the result, not the journey | "[Surprising result] — yeh mujhe khud believe nahi ho raha." |
| 2 (before) | Establish what it was like before | "6 mahine pehle main [problem state]. Bilkul wahi wali situation." |
| 3 (the change) | What they started doing | "Tab mujhe [product/app] mila. Honestly laga yeh bhi flop hoga." |
| 4 (evidence) | The result that made them believe | "Pehle [X] mein hi [specific metric] — tab mujhe lagaa yeh actually kaam karta hai." |
| 5 (direct CTA) | Clear, direct call to action | "Link bio mein hai. Try karo — free hai [/pehle mahine free hai]." |

**Tone markers:**
- Start energetic / disbelieving → builds to confident
- Specific numbers always beat vague claims
- "mujhe khud believe nahi ho raha" signals genuine surprise, not scripted enthusiasm

## Archetype 4: Demo / How-It-Works

**When to use:** App demos, SaaS tools, feature launches, when the product experience is the hook.

**Avatar recommendation:** `professional` or `tech-savvy`, often matched to the product's ICP.

**Chunk structure:**

| Chunk | Role | Example text |
|---|---|---|
| 1 (problem setup) | Name a pain point precisely | "Mujhe [specific frustrating task] mein itna time jaata tha — ek ghanta akele sirf [step]." |
| 2 (introduce the tool) | Name the product and its core action | "[Product] mein bas [core action]. That's it." |
| 3 (step-by-step, chunk 1) | First action in the flow | "[Action 1] karo. [Simple description of what happens]." |
| 4 (step-by-step, chunk 2) | Outcome of the action | "Result? [What they see]. [Seconds/minutes] mein." |
| 5 (compare + CTA) | Old way vs. new way close | "Jo pehle [old time] leta tha, ab [new time]. Link below." |

**Tone markers:**
- Matter-of-fact, not salesy
- "bas" (just) and "itna hi" (that's all) reduce friction perception
- Concrete time comparisons work better than percentages for Indian audiences

## Shared Writing Rules (All Archetypes)

1. **Each chunk must be self-contained.** If someone only sees one chunk, it should still make partial sense.
2. **The hook chunk (chunk 1) is the most important.** It's shown in the first 8 seconds and decides whether the user keeps watching.
3. **Hindi-English code switching is natural and encouraged** for urban Indian audiences. Don't force either language.
4. **Contractions and informal speech** ("toh", "yaar", "sach mein", "honestly") outperform formal language.
5. **Keep sentences short inside each chunk.** One thought per sentence. Break compound clauses.
6. **Avoid superlatives** ("best", "number 1", "game changer") unless they're backed by a specific claim in the same chunk.
7. **No staged enthusiasm.** Phrases like "This changed my life!" read as scripted. Specific facts create more authentic enthusiasm.

|---|---|
